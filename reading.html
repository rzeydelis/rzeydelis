How to Win Friends & Influence People by Dale Carnegie

While the title might sound sleezy (sorry for the lack of a better word), this book is truly amazing. It really helps you change the way you behave (in a good way, not a sleezy way). By understanding human rationale, you can getpeopleto appreciate you much more. Hands-down one of my favorite books ever because of how much I have learned from it.


Zero to One by Peter Thiel with Blake Masters

Peter Thiel is one of the greatest tech investors of today. He co-founded Paypal and was an early investor in Facebook. Peter Thiel explains the importance of innovating rather than copying business models and how to take your start-up from 0 to 1. This book has truly changed the way I think about business and how to bring value to society.


Reading List

Intelligent Investor by Benjamin Graham

Great book for those trying to understand and start investing. Also provides a lot of life philosophies that I follow today. Benajmin Graham was Warren Buffett's professor at Columbia University.


Benjamin Franklin: An American Life by Walter Isaacson

Benjamin Franklin was a great entrepreneur, inventor, diplomat and philanthropist during his time. One of the most interesting people to ever exist, I highly recommend this book to any aspiring entrepreneurs. Even if you are not an aspiring entrepreneur, this book is definitely worth a read.


Elon Musk biography by Ashlee Vance

Elon Musk is one of world's most revolutionary leaders. He is the CEO of Tesla (which consists of Solar City), SpaceX and the Boring Company. Known for his great memory and high intelligence, he manages 3 industry changing companies.


Predictive Analytics: Who will click, buy, lie or die by Eric Siegel

Great introduction to Predictive Analytics without all the math and stats. If you have been hearing this buzz phrase and you want to dive into Predictive Analytics, then this is the book for you.
